class="copy-heading"></span>What is DASH?</h2>

<p>Dash cryptocurrency, like <a href="/crypto/how-does-bitcoin-work"><strong>Bitcoin</strong></a>, is <strong>a digital currency</strong> <strong>that can be used to send or receive payments</strong>. In fact, Dash was built on top of <a href="/crypto/tutorials/blockchain-explained"><strong>the blockchain technology</strong></a> that Bitcoin uses, however, some significant improvements have been made to it.</p>

<p>Dash has gained popularity because it offers <strong>better privacy and higher transaction speeds</strong> than Bitcoin. It can achieve this with the help of a unique model that involves &ldquo;<em>Masternodes</em>&rdquo;.</p>

<p><em>I will elaborate more on Masternodes later as they form the backbone of the Dash network.</em></p>

<p><img style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="" src="/crypto/storage/media/images/dash-cryptocurrency-get-goin.o.jpg" alt="DASH Cryptocurrency: Complete Guide" title="DASH Cryptocurrency: Complete Guide" width="680" height="260" /></p>

<p>Not only does Dash cryptocurrency provide these benefits, but it also operates on <em>a self-governing and self-funding model</em>. Unlike Bitcoin, Dash&rsquo;s network can fund itself to make further improvements on the DASH technology.</p>

<p>Dash, where &lsquo;<strong>Dash</strong>&rsquo; is a mixture of the words &lsquo;<em>Digital</em>&rsquo; and &lsquo;<em>Cash</em>&rsquo;, was released on January 18, 2014, as &ldquo;Xcoin (XCO)&rdquo; by Evan Duffield. On January 28, 2014, the name was changed to &ldquo;Darkcoin&rdquo; before finally being renamed <a href="https://www.dash.org/forum/threads/the-birth-of-darkcoin.162/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">&ldquo;Dash&rdquo;</a> on March 25, 2015.</p>

<p>Dash was <em>forked</em> from <a href="/crypto/what-is-litecoin"><strong>Litecoin</strong></a>, and Litecoin was <em>forked</em> from Bitcoin. A bug got introduced when the fork happened, resulting in the mining of 1.9 million DASH coins within the first two days. <em>1.9 million is about 10% of the total Dash coins that will ever exist!</em></p>

<div class="note"><span style="color: #236fa1;"><em><strong>Note:</strong> A &ldquo;fork&rdquo; is when changes are made to a blockchain which causes the blockchain to split into two. On this occasion, Litecoin created Dash. Litecoin was also a fork of Bitcoin. So, all of there technology is similar, but each has its own unique features.</em></span></div>

<p>After this technical error, Evan Duffield offered to relaunch the coin, but the Dash community disapproved of the proposal, and so the project continued as is.</p>

<p>Since its launch in 2014, the DASH cryptocurrency was priced at <strong>less than $10</strong> until the end of 2016. As you can see from the chart below, the price of Dash started to increase at the beginning of 2017, and reached a high of <strong><a href="https://coinmarketcap.com/currencies/dash/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">$1540</a></strong> on the 20<sup>th</sup> December 2017.</p>

<p><em>So, now you know how it all started for the Dash cryptocurrency</em>. Next, let&rsquo;s look at each of the above-mentioned factors in detail to understand what makes Dash so unique.</p>

<h2 id="how-can-dash-be-used"><span class="copy-heading"></span>How Can DASH be Used?</h2>

<p>Dash has many features that provide it an edge over other cryptocurrencies like <strong>Bitcoin</strong> and <strong>Litecoin</strong>. Dash can be used to <strong>make transactions in a more private and speedy manner</strong> because of these three features: <strong>Masternodes</strong>, <strong>PrivateSend</strong>, and <strong>InstantSend</strong>.</p>

<p><em>Let&rsquo;s have an in-depth look at each one of them.</em></p>

<h3 id="masternodes"><span class="copy-heading"></span>Masternodes</h3>

<p>On the Bitcoin blockchain there are <strong>miners</strong> who verify transactions that take place on the blockchain, and in turn, get rewarded for their work. Similarly, on the Dash cryptocurrency&nbsp;blockchain, there are also miners, but the system is divided into two parts &mdash; <strong>Masternodes</strong> and <strong>miners</strong>.</p>

<p><em>A user must make a deposit of a minimum of 1000 DASH in order to become a Masternode. </em></p>

<p>Masternodes act as <em>special servers</em> that perform the critical functions on the Dash crypto network. They are responsible for <strong>Private Transactions</strong> (<em>PrivateSend</em>), <strong>Instant transactions</strong> (<em>InstantSend</em>), and the governance and <em>treasury system</em>.</p>
